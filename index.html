Stage 1 · Ages 6–10 recommended

Complete beginners

Kids who have never held a racket before. Focus is fun, confidence, and basic motor skills.

🏃
Warmup exercises
▶
1
Animal walk relay — crab walk, bear crawl, bunny hops across the court. Gets the body warm and kids laughing immediately.
3 min · full court · teams of 3–4
2
Balloon keep-up — each kid gets a balloon to hit upward with an open palm. Trains eye-hand coordination without fear of shuttles.
2 min · stationary
3
Follow the leader — coach leads side shuffles, high knees, arm circles and kids copy. Sneaks in athletic movement patterns.
3 min · follow line
4
Wrist circles + shoulder rolls — seated on court floor, gentle joint prep before they pick up rackets.
2 min · seated
Coach tip: Never call it "warming up" — call it "the mission" or "unlocking your powers." Kids engage far better with a narrative.
🏸
Equipment & grip basics
▶
1
Choosing the right racket — use lightweight junior rackets (70–80g). The grip should be thin enough for small hands. Check that fingers don't fully wrap around.
One-time setup · check each session
2
The handshake grip — teach forehand grip by saying "shake hands with the racket." The V between thumb and index sits on the top bevel. Loose and relaxed — no squeezing.
5 min · demonstrate + individual check
3
Grip pressure exercise — ask kids to grip as hard as they can (white knuckles), then release to 40%. This teaches the feel of a relaxed grip instantly.
2 min · partner check
4
Balancing the shuttle on strings — place a shuttle on the strings and have kids walk the court without dropping it. Great racket awareness exercise.
3 min · independent
🎯
Basic drills
▶
1
Tap-ups (self rally) — stand still and tap the shuttle upward continuously. Start with forehand only. Count personal best each session.
5 min · personal best challenge
2
Drop feed rally — coach drops shuttle from shoulder height, kid hits it back gently. No placement pressure — just making contact.
8 min · 1-on-1 with coach or helper
3
Target toss — place hula hoops on the court. Kids stand at the service line and underarm-hit shuttles into hoops. Intro to aim.
5 min · 5 shuttles each
4
Net tap drill — stand close to the net, gently tap shuttle over from a low feed. Teaches the feel of clearing the net with minimal force.
5 min · partner drill
Coach tip: Never correct more than one thing at a time. Pick the most important error, fix it, then move on. Too many notes overwhelms kids instantly.
🎮
Fun games
▶
1
Shuttle Bowling — set up plastic bottles on one end. Kids hit shuttles to knock them down. Use a low tee to start. Instant gratification!
10 min · teams, 3 turns each
2
Freeze Rally — kids hit a self-rally. When coach shouts "FREEZE!" everyone stops in exactly the pose they are in. Funny poses = laughs = motivation.
5 min · whole group
3
Castle Defend — build a "castle" from cones. One kid defends with their racket. Others throw foam balls. Racket handling without pressure.
8 min · rotating roles
Coach tip: Celebrate effort louder than outcomes. "Wow, you really kept your eye on that one!" beats "Good shot!" every time.
🧘
Cooldown
▶
1
Slow-motion walk — walk slowly across the court in "slow motion" like they're in a movie. Heart rate drops naturally.
1 min
2
Seated hamstring stretch — sit, legs out, reach for toes. Hold 15 seconds. Teach kids to breathe out as they reach forward.
1 min
3
Shoulder cross stretch — pull one arm across the chest, hold 10 seconds each side. Critical after racket use.
1 min
4
Star of the session — ask every kid to name one thing a teammate did well. Builds team culture and ends on a high.
2 min · seated circle
Stage 2 · 3–6 months of experience

Developing players

Kids who can rally, know the basic grip, and understand court boundaries. Focus shifts to technique and footwork.

🏃
Warmup exercises
▶
1
Court ghost footwork — without shuttle, shadow the 6 corners in sequence. Builds the movement memory that underpins all badminton footwork.
3 min · mirror coach demo
2
Ladder drills — use an agility ladder for in-in-out-out and single-foot hops. Improves quickness and leg coordination.
3 min · 2–3 passes each
3
Partner mirror drill — one kid leads lateral shuffles, partner mirrors exactly 1m apart. Trains reactive speed and focus.
2 min · switch leader halfway
4
Racket dynamic stretch — with racket in hand, arm circles forward/backward, figure-8 wrist rolls, and slow overhead swings. Warms up the entire hitting chain.
2 min
🏸
Technique development
▶
1
Forehand clear — introduce the throwing action. Elbow leads, snap the wrist at contact. Use the phrase "throw your hand at the shuttle."
10 min · feed drill + pairs
2
Backhand basics — teach the thumb-up backhand grip. Practice net lifts from the backhand side. Takes weeks to feel natural — be patient.
8 min · shadow swing + slow feeds
3
Low service — introduce short, low serve. Shuttle should skim the net and land in front of service line. Pendulum swing from waist height.
8 min · target markers
4
Ready position — feet shoulder-width apart, racket up at chest height, weight on balls of feet. Drill: coach points left/right, kid shuffles and returns.
5 min · reaction drill
Coach tip: Film a short clip and show kids their swing. Self-seeing creates faster correction than verbal instruction alone. Kids love watching themselves.
🎯
Drills with purpose
▶
1
Multi-shuttle feed drill — coach feeds 5 shuttles rapidly to different spots. Kid hits each and returns to center. Builds footwork + shot combo.
10 min · 3 rounds of 5
2
Straight clear rally — pairs hit clear shots straight down the line, cooperatively. Aim for 10+ consecutive shots. Count the rally.
8 min · count the rally
3
Net kill intro — coach feeds a loose net shuttle, kid pushes it steeply downward. Teach "don't flick — push flat and down."
5 min · slow feeds only
4
Service box challenge — mark targets in the service courts. Each serve attempt scores points for landing in different zones. Keep a scorecard for the season.
5 min · personal scoring
🎮
Fun games
▶
1
King/Queen of the Court — winner stays on, loser rotates out. Play short 5-point games. Creates gentle competition and teaches kids to bounce back.
15 min · full group
2
Zone battle — divide court into 3 zones. Points are worth more the deeper into the opponent's court you land a shot. Teaches court awareness without explaining it.
10 min · pairs
3
Call shot game — before serving, player must call where they'll land it ("net!" or "back!"). Correct = 2 points. In but wrong = 1 point. Builds intention.
8 min · pairs
Coach tip: Introduce a "skill sticker chart." Kids earn a sticker for each new skill they demonstrate. Visible progress is incredibly motivating.
🧘
Cooldown
▶
1
Slow shadow footwork — move through the 6-corner pattern at 20% speed. Active recovery + movement pattern reinforcement in one.
2 min
2
Hip flexor lunge stretch — lunge forward, back knee on ground, hold 20 seconds each side. Essential for lateral court movers.
2 min
3
Forearm + wrist stretch — arm extended, palm down, gently press the back of the hand with the opposite hand. 15 seconds each side.
1 min
4
One thing I'll work on — each kid tells the coach one thing they want to improve next session. Creates ownership and gives coach a preview.
2 min
Stage 3 · 6+ months of experience

Advancing players

Kids who can rally consistently and know basic strokes. Time to build tactical thinking, power, and competitive mindset.

🏃
Warmup exercises
▶
1
Competitive ghost sprints — coach shouts a corner number (1–6), kids sprint to it from center T and return. Race format. Loser does 3 mini squats.
3 min · race format
2
Jump rope or skipping — 60 seconds of light skipping. Elevates heart rate and improves foot speed and rhythm. Classic for a reason.
2 min
3
Partner racket tap rally — start rallying immediately in pairs as warm-up. Keep the shuttle low and controlled. Glides mentally into practice.
3 min · cooperative, no score
4
Full dynamic stretch chain — leg swings (10 each), hip openers, arm windmills, lateral lunge walks. Full-body prep for intense drills ahead.
3 min · in sequence
🏸
Advanced technique
▶
1
Smash mechanics — jump position, contact point above and ahead, steep angle. Feed high clears and let them smash. Emphasize contact point over power — power follows.
10 min · feed + shadow + live
2
Drop shot (slice) — same swing as a clear, but slice the shuttle at the last moment. Disguise is the key concept — the deception is the shot.
8 min · pairs + coach feed
3
Crosscourt control — rally crosscourt forehands, then backhands. Teaches directional control and sets up tactical thinking.
8 min · themed rally
4
High backhand clear — the hardest basic shot. Teach the round-the-head option as an alternative. Both are valid; kids should know both exist.
8 min · scaffold from shadow
Coach tip: Show short video clips of junior international players on a tablet. Seeing elite young players — not adults — performing the same shots is massively inspiring.
🎯
Tactical drills
▶
1
Clear-drop pattern drill — pairs alternate: one hits a clear, other hits a drop. No deviating. Trains the most common rally pattern in junior badminton.
10 min · cooperative then competitive
2
3-shot pattern play — coach sets a 3-shot combo (e.g. serve → lift → smash). Kid executes it 10 times. Introduces pattern thinking before free play.
10 min · coach-designed patterns
3
Pressure multi-shuttle — coach fires 10 shuttles rapidly to all corners. Kid must return each and recover to center between shots. Builds real match fitness.
8 min · 3 rounds with rest
4
Serve return practice — one kid serves repeatedly, partner focuses only on quality returns. Swap after 10 serves. A neglected skill in youth coaching.
8 min · dedicated focus
🎮
Competitive games
▶
1
Mini-tournament (9-point sets) — run a round-robin with proper scoring. Use a whiteboard leaderboard. Teach score-calling and fair play before results.
20 min · full structure
2
Tactical challenge match — each player gets a secret "mission" before play (e.g. "win every point from a smash"). Teaches intention during play.
10 min · with debrief
3
Coaching timeout game — each kid gets 1 "timeout" card per match. They can pause and ask for 1 piece of advice. Teaches self-awareness and using resources.
10 min · pairs
Coach tip: Debrief every competitive game. Ask "what shot kept getting you in trouble?" before giving your own answer. Kids who self-diagnose fix problems 3× faster.
🧘
Cooldown
▶
1
Gentle rally to wind down — 2 minutes of slow, cooperative netting. Physiological cooldown while keeping a racket in hand.
2 min
2
Full lower body stretch sequence — standing quad stretch (30s each leg), seated butterfly (20s), lying hamstring pull (20s each).
3 min
3
Breathing reset — 3 deep breaths in through nose (4 counts), out through mouth (6 counts). Transfers directly to pre-match nerves control.
1 min
4
Session reflection card — each kid says: one strength, one area to improve, one goal for next session. Builds growth mindset from early on.
2 min
